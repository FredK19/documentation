Converting from AuctionWorks to Zoovy

Data Import

If you're feeling exceptionally lazy you can might consider simply requesting an import of your running eBay auctions. Please read the details on importing listings from eBay.com Click Here This probably won't get all your data, but at least it will provide a starting point.
Once you have exported your Customers and/or Products using the Export feature in AuctionWorks you will need to remove AuctionWorks header and replace it with a Zoovy header. AuctionWorks regularly changes it's export format, and so it's very difficult (if not impossible) to keep this file up to date. In general it is safe to assume that you can ignore new fields by simply leaving the header field blank.

If you need assistance please contact your assigned implementation technician.

Auction Works regularly changes it's export format. We recommend you import the AuctionWorks CSV file into Excel and verify the column orders are still correct. Zoovy makes no warranty with regards to accuracy of this information.

    • Getting Acclimated

  • AuctionWorks calls them "Auctions" whereas Zoovy calls them "Channels"
  • LaunchBots are called "Scheduled Channels"
  • You'll find a lot of the equivalent reports that AuctionWorks offers in the Order Manager - we do not offer web based equivalents.

    Avoiding Culture Shock:
    In general you'll find Zoovy is much more flexible than AuctionWorks with regards to how "you want to run your business" - but this is not necessarily a good thing! In our experience AuctionWorks offers a program which is designed to be a "one size fits all sellers" - this is particularly well suited for getting sellers started. Unfortunately as sellers become more and more advanced they find the "one size fits all" approach more restrictive and cumbersome. Zoovy was built with fundamentally different assumptions than AuctionWorks, we are much more focused on branding, and encouraging repeat business. Zoovy looks at Auctions such as eBay as a terrific way to generate additional brand exposure and find new customers, not necessarily as "a pure auction tool" (not that you can't use Zoovy that way, but it's important to understand we started from a different place).

    Zoovys #1 priority in design is flexibility and the ability to adapt to specific business models. At the same time a lot of sellers migrating from AuctionWorks find Zoovys unstructured approach towards listings very frustrating, especially when compared to AuctionWorks highly structured and rigid approach. For this we can only offer the following advice:

    Just remember why you're switching! There is nothing you can do on AuctionWorks that you can't do on Zoovy - whereas there are lots of things you can do with Zoovy that you can't do with AuctionWorks! Be patient - be willing to "think outside the box" and you'll do fine!
