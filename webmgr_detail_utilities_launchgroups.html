Launch Groups

Overview

Launch Groups allow you to syndicate products from categories, lists, or hardcoded csv lists of products into marketplaces at predictable days/times.

Getting Help

The Launch Groups manager has inline help - just click the "Info" link adajcent to any field to learn how to properly configure the values.

Queuing Strategies

Launch Groups support a variety of "Queueing Strategies" - a detailed explanation of each one is provided below:
If you set the Max Products per launch to either -1 (unlimited) or a value greater than the number of products in the group then the queuing strategy is irrelevant since all products will be launched every time.

Round Robin

This selects products in a sequential order. Each Launch Group keeps an internal count of how many listings it has performed, which is then divided by the number of products in the list and the remainer determines the beginning offset in the product list. When the end of the list is reached it starts over at the beginning.
In Round Robin - a products position in the list determines its order in the launch. If the products position changes (e.g. items are added or removed from the list, or the sequence is resorted in a different order) then products may be either passed over, or launched duplicately. Round Robin is almost certainly not the best queuing strategy for highly contentious lists of products whose order and sequence change frequently.

Sell Thru

This data goes to the specific marketplace and pulls the total number of listings, and total number of sales over the past 90 days. A simple ratio is computed and the items with the highest sell thru ratio are used. If an item has no sales record, it's sell thru percentage is set at 100% so it will sell.

In Stock

The "In Stock" queuing strategy looks at the number in stock, removes reserve inventory and then uses those items which have the highest available quantity for sale.

Recent

The "Recent" queuing strategy looks at the modified times for each product. A product is modified anytime it is saved or edited in any way.

Least Recently Used

This attempts to find the total number of listings for all marketplaces, and launch those items which are NOT being listed as frequently (and therefore may not have been sold).
