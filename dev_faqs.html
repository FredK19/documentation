Utilities: Frequently Asked Questions

Overview

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) are supported in Zoovy through our faq engine, which allows your customers to easily search by keyword or topic. The FAQ element is available on the newer layouts for the 'returns' page.

How to Create FAQ's

The first step in creating your faq library is to create a topic. An example of a topic may be 'shipping' or 'payment' or a specific product id. Create a few topics first.
How to create FAQ topics
  • Log in to your account at www.zoovy.com.
  • Click on the utilities tab.
  • Click on the Frequently Asked Questions link
  • Under the add content header, enter a topic into the topic prompt
  • Push the 'add topic' button.

    • Now that you have your topics created, you are ready to enter some questions, answers, and keywords.
    How to create FAQs
  • Log in to your account at www.zoovy.com.
  • Click on the utilities tab.
  • Click on the Frequently Asked Questions link.
  • Under the add content header, select a topic from the select list.
  • Enter a short question.
  • Enter a detailed but short answer (nobody is going to want to read a book here).
  • Enter keywords.
  • Push the 'Add FAQ' button.
    • Keywords
    Your keywords should be a comma separated list of pertinent words. Example:
    Q: How do you ship your items
    A: Via FedEx. We offer priority, ground and two day shipping.
    Keywords: fedex, ship, shipping, ground, delivery, priority, ground

    Displaying the faq's on the website

    To display the faq's, you need to select the appropriate layout on your returns page. Currently, the only layout to support this technology is r-20061003: Policies Page: 3 policies and faq. Custom Layouts can be used on other pages in your store if you want the faq engine elsewhere.

    FAQ Strategy

    Be precise with your question and answer

    Questions should be to the point. This is even more true for answers. Avoid open ended questions that are going to require a long explanation. If you find yourself writing a long answer, most likely you need to break your question into multiple questions. A simple 'yes' or 'no' answer is perfectly acceptable and your customers are likely to appreciate the directness.

    The keyword in FAQ is 'Frequently'

    Don't use your FAQ as an 'everything you ever wanted to know about my website'. Keep it limited to the questions you are asked most often. The purpose of an FAQ is to reduce your customer support costs. If the page is overwhelming, people are less likely to spend time sorting to find the info.

    Keep it relevant

    The faq is a place to address customer's questions, not to try to upsell or market to them. Use the FAQ as it was intended for the highest impact. Use actual FAQ from your customers, not questions you think they want the answers to.

    Update!

    If the FAQ works for your customers, they'll stop asking you the questions on the faq, but will start asking you questions that are not on the faq. When you are continually asked the same question, update your faq.

    Product specific questions

    Avoid product specific questions, as this will likely bloat your faq. These types of questions should be answered on the product detail page or within a product guide (installation instructions, assembly instructions, etc).

    FAQ Manager

    Use our FAQ manager, not a layout that is just a big block of text. Our FAQ manager will display the topics, questions and answers in a logical format that makes it easy for the customer to find what they are looking for and is also search-able.
