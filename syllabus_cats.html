Syllabus: Categories and Lists

Categories and Lists Training

This basic training will teach you how to build categories for your online store.
A "category" in Zoovy terminology is a page for your online store.

Categories Overview

We advise to wait until you have settled on how your custom designed website will look before creating your categories.

Safe vs Pretty Names

The "Safe" name is the first name that you give a category. The "Pretty" name is what is shown on your webstore to your customers. You create the Pretty name by using the Rename button under the category after it has been created.
Your categories are ordered by the first name that you gave them.

You can name your categories after a keyword that you want people to use to find your site for better search engine optimization.
Safe names with spaces will default to underscores. Use (-) to create more SEO friendly URL's. example: www.domain.com/category/keyword-keyword/

Product Finder

The Product Finder assists you in locating products that you would like to add to a page. The Product Finder is found in various parts of Zoovy, such as the Related Items section.
How to add products to a page
  • Click on Setup in the primary navigation.
  • Click on Categories and Lists.
  • Find the category to which you would like to add products.
  • Click the Add Product button beneath that category.
  • Use the Product Finder to locate products in your Zoovy account to add.
  • Check the boxes adjacent to the products you would like to add to the category.

    • Lists

    A list is a special kind of category. It is group of products assembled for a specific purpose.

    Hidden Categories

    You can make it so that a category does not appear in your online store by renaming it with an exclamation mark in front of its name (for example, "!Holiday Specials").

    Root Level Category vs Product List

    A Root Level Category is a page on your web store. This page can be visible in the primary navigation or hidden so that it can only be accessed through a hyperlink, domain name or banner (as a few examples).
    A Product List is constructed just as you would a Root Level Category but no page is created. Lists are groups of products assembled for some special purpose. Some examples might be a list for items that you want to use on a specific marketplace syndication or a list of best sellers that you want to display in a special box on your homepage.

    Homework

    Build your category structure as you would like to see it on your Zoovy webstore. Even if you do not plan to focus on your web store, creating your navigation and placing products in categories will help you better utilize marketplaces such as eBay and Amazon. It is an important step for any Zoovy user.
