Website Usability

Concept Overview

This document provides an overview of a list of some of the best practices for your eCommerce and shopping cart usability. This can greatly effect the success and ability to generate regular sales and increase them over time. You want the shopper to have the best experience possible and reduce the abandonment. Even some of the simplest changes can increase the results of your online store. It is not always necessary to implement everything. You should decide and test the ones that produce the best results for you.
Can be used as a guide for our Extended Marketing Analysis on website usablity.

Usability Best Practices

Using Headlines / Subheading / Breadcrumb Navigation

Providing text headings and subheadings can help customers determine what page they are viewing. Breadcrumbs help customers determine how deep they are in a site. Providing this info makes it easy to view before and after page. By showing customers what they are viewing you are making the site more usable, and enabling them to find what they are looking for more effectively. This also help with search engines, and helping your pages to be easily found and indexed.

Contact Information Visibility

Make sure you contact information and phone numbers are easy for customers to find, and located above the page fold. They can find your products and purchase them online, but this could help increase email and phone leads. There will be consumers who prefer to do orders over the phone or email, and without this information visible, you could potentially be missing out. By making this information visible it makes the site more usable for those customers. It can also play a role in there confidence in placing orders if the can easily find contact information for the merchant. It should be consistently located in the same place on every page.

Site Search Function

All sites should have a search function of some kind. It can just be a simple search or more advanced depending on your products. It must be easy for them to spot, and available to customers at all times. They are typically located near the top above the fold, but can sometimes be included on a sidebar.
You should always test your site search to ensure it is working properly, and review your search logs periodically to determine if you need to make any changes

Search Results Display

Make sure that your search results display the products and allow for further sorting. The # of product results should be displayed as well as what the search was for. Allow shoppers to show a designated number of items per page. Example of further sorting viewing lowest to highest price, by color, etc.

Related Products/Cross Selling

Grouping products to show related item & other items of interest can help lower cart abandonment. Having similar items they can add at checkout with ease can also help increase the total purchase. By grouping items that are related and cross selling you are making it easier for a customer to find products related to what they are interested in purchasing.

Product Page Copy

Your product copy is important for customer usability and search engines. This includes your text heading and descriptions. The headlines, copy, message, buttons, calls to action all influence the behavior of the customer. It determines whether they purchase, create account, move through site, or leave the site altogether. You should be answering any questions that customers might have for the product or category. The headlines should simple and direct, and clearly show what a customer will be able to perform on the page. Use of keywords are important in heading and the entire copy. Try to use targeted keywords throughout. Write compelling copy that includes main points or highlights Use content that can help persuade them to buy the product. Ex. Kayak 121D - Model Kayak 121D is great for lake and ocean kayaking. It is also lightweight for easy transport.

Product Availablity

If you show products that are out of stock, make sure to show these as soon as possible. The customer should know a product is unavailable before they get to the cart. Just make sure it is before the checkout process.

Add to Cart Buttons

Ensure that the Add to Cart buttons are very visible. Use a different font and make it larger. If they do not stand out in some way

Checkout/Page Elements

Keep pages as clutter free as possible. Make sure your landing pages are relevant for what is being displayed. The checkout flow should be smooth and easy, avoid any unnecessary navigation or buttons. By making the pages appealing and easy to navigate you increase the chances of consumers purchasing.

Pricing/Discounts

Make sure you are displaying the product price as well as any other cost associated with the products. Shoppers want to see what their totals are with discounts, shipping, taxes. By allowing this to be seen as they add to the cart it reduces abandonment that would happen if they only say it right before purchasing. Make sure they are aware of any discounts and can see that in the cart as well.

Cart Access

Make sure that the cart is easily accessible and changes can be made easily. Is it difficult to remove an item from the cart. Have a mini cart ensure that the customer can see the cart content without having to go directly to the cart page. This increases usablility because they do not have to return to the cart page while still shopping.

Return Policy Make sure that your return policy is clearly defined and easily found. It is advised to be accessible from all pages. This is important for an online store, since the customer can not physically hold the product before purchasing. This has a great impact on their decision to purchase.

Payment/Shipping Options

Offer a variety of payment and shipping options. Shoppers are hesitant to checkout when purchasing if they do not see their preferred payment method. Some shoppers only use certain shipping companies. By offering a variety you are ensuring the usablity for your shoppers. Many shoppers will leave the cart of they realize you do not offer their method. (Credit, Paypal, Google Checkout, eCheck, COD, etc)

Security Icons

You want your customers to know they are purchasing from a safe and secure site which is protecting their confidential information. This can help increase your sales and also help increase purchases at a higher price point.
