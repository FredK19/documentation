New Domain Survival Guide

New Domain Overview

This guide is intended to help you register a new domain in Zoovy. Use this guide if you do not currently own a domain and wish to purchase one directly through Zoovy.
Register Domain
  • 1. Setup Zoovymail.
  • 2. Register domain.
  • 3. Configure domain.
    • It is very important that you complete each step and complete them in the proper order.

    WAIT!

    Did you fill out your Company Information completely? You should have done this back when you were doing the basic setup of your account, but double-check just in case. Go to Setup > Site Builder and Edit your Company Information. If this is not filled out, you are not going to be able to complete the following steps.

    Step 1: Setup Zoovymail

    A newly registered domain will appear in your Zoovymail account, if you have one. Please take a moment to ensure that you have Zoovymail enabled before registering a new domain. See Doc 50268 for steps to provisioning a Zoovymail account if you need further help with this step.

    Step 2: Register Domain

    Go to Setup > Domain Hosting and click on the Add Domains button. On the following screen, click on the Register button. Enter the domain that you want without the leading "www." For example, if you wanted your domain to be "www.zoovy.com" you would just enter "zoovy.com"

    Step 3: Configure Domain

    Once you have the domain entered, it will appear under Setup > Domain Hosting > Domain Config. You will want to Edit Configuration on your newly purchased domain. Select the appropriate Email and Hosting mode and save.
    If you simply want a domain that points to your Zoovy web store, your Email should be "Zoovymail 2.0" and your Domain Mode should be "(DEFAULT) Default Hosting."

    WHOIS Data

    If you're transferring a domain, all the previous information (except the technical contact) will be maintained.
    For new domain registrations the system will automatically load whatever information is in the default profile in your Zoovy store.
    Zoovy staff can edit the whois data after the fact to make it say whatever you want.
    The technical contact information will point at our operations staff.
